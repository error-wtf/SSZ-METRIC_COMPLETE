# SSZ Observable Limitations and Research Caveats

This document registers honest, scientific limitations of the canonical Xi-primary Segmented Spacetime (SSZ) metric research framework.

## Honest Research Limitations

1. **Not a General Theory of Relativity (GR) Replacement**:
   The current framework provides a static, spherically symmetric coordinate metric. It is a research framework for exploring regularization, not a complete dynamical alternative field theory.

2. **No Physical Source-Formation Matter Model**:
   While the regularized metric avoids coordinate singularities, the exact physical matter fields or source-formation mechanics (how a segmented field is formed dynamically from matter) are separate research tasks and not solved.

3. **No Non-Linear Dynamical Stability Proof**:
   The stability of the metric under arbitrary non-linear dynamic perturbations is currently unproven and is an active topic of research.

4. **Kerr Metric Excluded from Core**:
   Rotating coordinates (Kerr equivalents) are excluded from the canonical pure core. Any rotating metrics or drag-coefficient tests exist solely in exploratory or legacy comparison folders.

5. **LIGO / GW Template-Bound Limitations**:
   Gravitational wave detections (LIGO/Virgo) are currently modeled against GR templates. Using posterior values from template-based fits as "independent proof" of alternative metrics is circular. Genuine SSZ gravitational wave validation requires raw strain data analysis and independent waveform templates, which are exploratory and not canonical.

6. **Pending External Observational Datasets**:
   Several high-energy and strong-field astrophysical domains (e.g., neutron star crusts, accretion disk margins) are registered as "external reference pending" and not claimed as fully validated.
